Understanding Your Carbon Footprint & How to Reduce It

Every single action you take — the light you switch on, the car you drive, the food you eat, the flight you book — leaves an invisible mark on our planet. That mark is called your carbon footprint.

Your choices today decide if your children
will breathe clean air and drink clean water tomorrow.

What Is Carbon Footprint?

Your carbon footprint is the total greenhouse gases (CO₂, methane, etc.) you are responsible for — directly or indirectly — measured in tons of CO₂ equivalent per year.

Average Global: ~4 tons | Average American: ~16 tons | Sustainable target by 2030: under 2 tons.
